# English Improvement Guide for Chinese-Speaking Developers

## Common Spelling Mistakes

| Wrong | Correct | Tip |
|-------|---------|-----|
| inprove | **improve** | im- prefix (not in-) |
| pricese | **precise** | pre-CISE (精确的) |
| communitation | **communication** | communicate → communication |
| explian | **explain** | ex-PLAIN |
| functoin | **function** | func-TION |

## Phrasing Patterns (中文思维 → 英文表达)

| Chinese-style | Natural English | Why |
|---------------|-----------------|-----|
| "use Chinese" | "communicate in Chinese" | "use" is too vague for languages |
| "words use method" | "word usage" / "vocabulary" | English prefers compound nouns |
| "daily talk" | "everyday conversation" | "talk" as noun is informal |
| "right spell" | "correct spelling" | adjective + gerund noun |
| "better explain" | "explain more clearly" | adverb modifies verb, not adjective |
| "give me improve advice" | "give me advice on improving" | preposition + gerund pattern |
| "how to pricese" | "how to be precise" | "precise" is adjective, needs "be" |

## Grammar Patterns to Watch

### 1. Missing "to" in verb chains
- Wrong: "enable a tool give me feedback"
- Right: "enable a tool **to** give me feedback"
- Pattern: `[verb1] + [object] + **to** + [verb2]`

### 2. Adjective vs Noun forms
- Wrong: "improve advice" (improve = verb)
- Right: "improvement advice" (improvement = noun)
- Pattern: noun + noun compounds need noun form

### 3. Article usage (a/the/zero)
- "explain the concept" (specific) vs "explain a concept" (any one)
- Zero article for uncountable: "give me advice" (not "give me an advice")

### 4. Sentence structure
- Chinese: topic-first, conditions-first → "please when you work, also do X"
- English: action-first → "While working on this, could you also do X?"

## Quick Self-Check Before Sending

1. Did I capitalize "I" and sentence starts?
2. Did I use periods/commas (not just spaces)?
3. Is every verb in the right form? (improve → improving / improvement)
4. Does each sentence have a subject + verb?
5. Spell-check: read it backwards word-by-word to catch typos
